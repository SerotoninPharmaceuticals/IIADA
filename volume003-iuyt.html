Introduction

[Internet infrastructure] is an array of remote hardware, software and wetware working to send and receive information to/from various systems. Every instance of a page loading on a computer's web browser, every browser advertisement squashing into your screen, a Really Simple Syndication (RSS) feed streaming into a reader, or a Voice over Internet Protocol (VoIP) call being made represents this complex internet infrastructure. Its basic components include data centers, networks, servers, storage devices, server applications, and the most important, users in front of their monitors

[Deepsea] is the lowest layer in the ocean, existing below the thermocline and above the seabed, at a depth of 1000 fathoms (1800 m) or more. Little or no light penetrates this part of the ocean and most of the organisms that live there rely for subsistence on falling organic matter produced in the photic zone. For this reason scientists once assumed that life would be sparse in the deep ocean but virtually every probe has revealed that, on the contrary, life is abundant in the deep ocean.[1]

[Anthropology] is the study of homo sapiens, past and present, that draws and builds upon knowledge from the social sciences and biological sciences, as well as the humanities and the natural sciences. [2]

It is essential to emphasize beforehand that since deepsea homo sapiens has been isolated far from continental homo sapiens, consequently, they varies a lot both biologically and culturally. Traditional anthropology research methods are no longer valid under such circumstance. Therefore, a new discipline - which is Deepsea Anthropology- must be founded in order to have a better understanding of this new homo sapiens subspecies, culturally and aesthetically within our current research scope.

The third volume of this exhibition will be focused on the social relations between individuals of deepsea homo sapiens. Trinkets being excessively used in their daily social life, which were believed to related to religious belief, will be exhibited to provides you a better insight into the theocratic social structure of deepsea homo sapiens. Presumably, those trinkets were mostly used as charms or talismans, judging from the fact that they all share the same shape, we can even further speculate that idolatrous worship still plays a vital role among deepsea homo sapiens, possibly towards a deity of flattery and laziness.

A Symbol of Faith and Hierarchy

In most social occasions, there would be a secondary trinket attached to the talisman, which was engraved with Arabic numerals. We believe it is a token symbolizing the holder's amount of faith as well as identifying his/her rank in the religious hierarchy.

A Pilgrimage to The Surface

Every other year, the most pious and bravest ones among deepsea homo sapiens will take a deadly pilgrimage to a sacred island somewhere above the Pacific Ocean, worshiping a monument of the deity they believe in.
97% of the pilgrims would get killed near the sea surface, due to the low pressure and sunlight. Such mass casualty usually results in a ocean phenomenon called red tide. The remaining successful 3% would normally be praised as saint once they made it back.

To the left is a statue we excavated from the island aforementioned, is it a standalone statue or collapsed part of a bigger one is still unknown to us.

A calligraphy work of a pictographic letter excavated from another tribe of the undersea society. The latest research revealed its nature of idolatry.

Talismans used by the pictographic letter-using tribe, despite the fact that their owner has been separated considerably far from other tribes both culturally and geographically, they still share a huge similarity with the talismans used by other tribes.
What reason could have caused this? We believe it is a living proof that all deepsea homo sapiens originate from the same ancestor biologically.
